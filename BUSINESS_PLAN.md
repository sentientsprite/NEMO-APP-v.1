## 1. Business Overview

### Mission

Prana Marketing Solutions is a one-person, autonomous marketing agency built to serve Salt Lake County small businesses — home services, medical/dental, legal, and restaurants. We deliver enterprise-quality digital marketing (SEO, Google Ads, content, email) at SMB-accessible prices, powered by AI agents running 24/7 on local infrastructure with zero cloud dependency.

### The Opportunity

West Jordan, South Jordan, Herriman, Sandy, and Taylorsville are home to thousands of owner-operated businesses that are invisible on Google. They have strong reviews and loyal customers but rank #4 or lower for their core service keywords — costing them an estimated $10,000–$50,000+ per year in missed leads. No agency in the area serves this segment with both the expertise and the pricing that makes sense for a $500K–$2M revenue business.

### Competitive Advantage

- **Local Focus** — We only serve Salt Lake County SMBs. We know the market, the competitors, and the search patterns.
- **Proven Results** — 300% conversion lifts, $56K revenue growth, 150% traffic increase. Actual client outcomes, not projections.
- **AI-Powered Ops** — 5 named agents and 12 specialist roles handle lead research, audits, content drafts, GBP optimization, review responses, and reporting. Clients get agency-quality work at freelancer prices.
- **Productized Add-Ons** — Three SKU'd services (Beacon, Echo, Bloom) clients can layer onto any tier. Predictable scope, predictable price, fast time-to-value.
- **No Lock-in** — Month-to-month after 90 days. Clients stay because results compound, not because they're trapped.
- **Transparent Pricing** — Three core tiers, three add-ons, no hidden fees, no retainer surprises.

### 2.2 Add-On Products

Three productized services Raymond can attach to any Flow client and which ship inside Prana by default. Each add-on has a fixed scope, fixed deliverables, and a single SKU price — designed for fast contracting and predictable margin.

#### Beacon — GBP Autopilot

**Problem.** Local businesses, especially multi-location ones, can't keep their Google Business Profile fresh, ranked, and consistent across directories. Every stale post, missed Q&A, and inconsistent NAP citation costs them Maps visibility and "near me" calls.

**What it does.** Beacon runs four specialist roles on a weekly cadence: Monitor (tracks local-pack rank, competitor GBPs, Q&A), Optimizer (drafts posts, photo updates, category and attribute changes), Review (drafts personalized brand-voice replies and triggers post-job review request sequences), and Citation (scans and fixes directory inconsistencies).

**Specialist agents.** Monitor, Optimizer, Review, Citation — all sub-roles inside Hunter (outbound monitoring) and Aria (client-facing approvals).

**Deliverables.**
- Weekly Local Visibility Score report (rank in local pack, share-of-voice vs top 3 competitors, review velocity, post engagement).
- 4 GBP posts per location per month, drafted, approved via PRANA-EXECUTE, then published.
- Q&A monitoring with drafted answers within 24 hours.
- Monthly citation cleanup log across the top 30 directories.
- Quarterly competitor pack audit.

**Pricing.** $129/mo for the first location. +$79/mo for each additional location (Carey-validate this number).

**Seasonal Campaign Pack add-on.** $299 one-time per pack. Examples: "Utah Spring Cleanup", "Winter Snow Removal", "Back-to-School Family Dental".

**Ideal client.** Multi-location service businesses (CTR Heating's 8 service areas, White Pine Dental's 2 locations, Carey's seasonal landscaping coverage).

#### Echo — Review Flywheel

**Problem.** 87% of consumers read reviews before hiring a contractor, dentist, or attorney. Most SMBs ask for reviews inconsistently, reply generically (or not at all), and miss the social-proof flywheel that compounds local SEO and trust.

**What it does.** Echo connects to a job-completion signal (webhook from invoicing tools, parsed completion email, or a manual "Job Done" button in the Prana dashboard). On trigger, it sends a personalized review request via SMS or email with a direct Google link. When reviews land, the Response specialist drafts a brand-voice reply that references the specific service, technician, or context. The Amplifier role tracks sentiment trends, surfaces service issues to the owner, and turns positive reviews into testimonial graphics and "before/after" social posts.

**Specialist agents.** Trigger, Response, Amplifier — sub-roles under Hunter (signal detection) and Aria (client-facing content).

**Deliverables.**
- Personalized review request SMS/email sequences (3-touch, capped at 1 per customer per 90 days).
- Drafted brand-voice replies on every new review, awaiting one-click approval.
- Monthly sentiment + trend report (themes, NPS-style scoring, action items).
- Social-proof content pack (4 testimonial graphics + 2 before/after captions per month).
- Optional "Thank You + Maintenance Plan Upsell" sequence for recurring-revenue verticals.

**Pricing.** $89/mo flat. +$0.40 per review request beyond 100/mo (validate-flat-vs-overage with first 2 clients).

**Ideal client.** Any business with 10+ reviews already and a steady flow of completed jobs. Best fit: dental practices, HVAC, plumbing, landscaping, personal injury law (where the post-case "thank you" timing is sensitive but valuable).

#### Bloom — Seasonal Content Engine

**Problem.** Owners know they should publish weekly, but they don't have the time or the local awareness (Salt Lake weather, ski-season traffic, Utah-specific holidays, vertical seasonality) to do it well. Generic AI content reads generic and ranks generic.

**What it does.** Bloom runs five specialist roles in sequence: Planner builds a rolling 90-day calendar tied to SLC weather, local events, and high-intent vertical keywords; Creator drafts Google Posts, social captions, and longer service-area blog posts; Visual generates or edits before/after images and seasonal graphics; Distributor publishes (or queues for approval) across approved channels; Performance feeds engagement and ranking data back into the next planning cycle.

**Specialist agents.** Planner, Creator, Visual, Distributor, Performance — sub-roles primarily under Hunter (planning + creation) and Aria (publishing approval and reporting).

**Deliverables.**
- Rolling 90-day content calendar, refreshed monthly.
- 4 Google Posts per week.
- 2 long-form blog posts per month, optimized for local + vertical keywords.
- 8 before/after or seasonal visuals per month.
- Monthly performance report (rankings, traffic, engagement) feeding the next plan.

**Pricing.** $249/mo. +$99/mo per additional channel beyond the default bundle (GBP + blog + 1 social network). Validate 2 vs 4 blog posts/mo at this price after first Bloom client.

**Ideal client.** Established service businesses with a website that ranks for at least one keyword and a willingness to publish weekly. Strong fit: home services with visible "before/after" potential (landscaping, roofing, dental cosmetic), and any business in a seasonally-driven vertical.

### The 5 Named Agents

- **Aria (CEO)** — Orchestrates all agents, consolidates weekly reports, escalates to Raymond, owns client-facing approval flow.
- **Hunter** — Lead generation, prospect auditing, outreach drafting, plus all add-on signal detection and content drafting. Runs every Monday and on add-on triggers.
- **Scout** — R&D ingestion. Analyzes tools, competitors, market signals.
- **Architect** — R&D synthesis. Turns Scout analysis into implementation blueprints.
- **Validator** — R&D testing. Verifies Architect blueprints against actual stack.

### Specialist Roles (inside the 5 agents)

The three add-ons add 12 specialist roles. They are roles, not new agents — each is a sub-process owned by Hunter or Aria. This keeps the 5-agent architecture clean while letting each add-on have a clear internal owner.

**Beacon roles** (4)
- *Monitor* — Hunter. Tracks local pack rank, competitor GBPs, Q&A.
- *Optimizer* — Hunter. Drafts GBP posts, photo updates, category and attribute changes.
- *Review* — Aria. Drafts brand-voice review replies, triggers post-job request sequences.
- *Citation* — Hunter. Scans and fixes directory NAP inconsistencies.

**Echo roles** (3)
- *Trigger* — Hunter. Watches job-completion webhooks and the dashboard "Job Done" button.
- *Response* — Aria. Drafts personalized review replies referencing job context.
- *Amplifier* — Aria. Sentiment trends, social-proof content, maintenance-plan upsell sequences.

**Bloom roles** (5)
- *Planner* — Hunter. Builds rolling 90-day calendar tied to SLC weather + vertical seasonality.
- *Creator* — Hunter. Drafts Google Posts, social captions, blog posts.
- *Visual* — Hunter. Generates or edits before/after images and seasonal graphics.
- *Distributor* — Aria. Publishes or queues for approval across approved channels.
- *Performance* — Aria. Tracks engagement and rankings, feeds learnings back into the next plan.

### Approval Gate (PRANA-EXECUTE)

Every Beacon GBP post, every Echo review reply, and every Bloom blog post passes through the PRANA-EXECUTE approval queue before publish. This matches the existing gate in the Prana runtime: agents write to `~/.openclaw/workspace/ops/pending/`, the dashboard shows pending approvals, Raymond approves in the dashboard. No money, email, or browser action ships without a human OK.

### Weekly Oversight (4–6 hours max)

- **Monday** — Review Hunter's lead list (20 min). Approve/edit outreach drafts (30 min). Send approved messages.
- **Tuesday** — Approve queued GBP posts and Bloom blog drafts (30 min).
- **Wednesday** — Check for prospect replies. Follow up or escalate warm leads (15 min). Approve queued review replies (15 min).
- **Friday** — Review prana-status dashboard. Check Anthropic spend. Review any agent reports (15 min).
- **Client work** — Review and approve Aria's deliverable drafts before sending to clients (1–2 hours per client per month).
